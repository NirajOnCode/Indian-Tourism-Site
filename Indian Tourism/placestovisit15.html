Places to visit

West Bengal has various visitor attractions including within the city.

West Bengal

Kolkata

KolkataKolkata is the capital of West Bengal and one of the four metropolitan cities of India. It has a strong hold on politics, culture and economy of the country. Despite the prevailing aspect of modernisation, Kolkata has successfully maintained its charm and beauty.

Darjeeling

DarjeelingWidely known as 'Queen of the Hills', Darjeeling will be your perfect option to get away for a few days to escape the mundaneness of daily life. It offers a colourful view of nature. You can also spot Mount Kanchenjunga if the sky remains clear. The six Tâ€™s that Darjeeling is known by are- Tea, Toy Train, Teak Forests, Tiger Hill, Touristic View and Trekker's paradise.

Sundarban

SundarbanSundarban is the world's largest known delta system. It came into existence because of three mighty rivers, Ganga, Meghna and Brahmaputra. Certain parts of Sundarban still remain unexplored. It is home to wide stretches of mangrove forest. Sundarban is also famous for its Royal Bengal Tiger, as well as the Tiger Reserve and National Park.

Siliguri

SiliguriSiliguri is one of the best places to visit in West Bengal. On visiting, you will come across the most breathtaking view of the snow-clad Himalayan Range. There are various attractions that invite tourists from across the globe!

Mirik

MirikThe weather, ease of access and amazing sceneries are the few reasons why Mirik has become quite a popular destination among tourists. Its distance from Darjeeling is about 49 km. Even Mt. Kanchenjunga is visible from here. The sunrises and sunsets are bliss to experience.

Digha

DighaLocated about 187 km Southwest of Kolkata, Digha is one of the favourite sea resorts of any tourist visiting Kolkata. It is not too crowded and has a shallow sand beach. The waves, too, are gentle and calming. When you reach there, just get in the waves and you will feel amazing.

Dooars

DooarsSituated in Northeast India, Dooars is a part of the foothills of the eastern Himalayan Range. The name has been derived from "duar", meaning door. There you will find 18 gateways through which Bhutanese people communicate with people living here. It has several sanctuaries and national parks that serve as a great tourist spot.

Kurseong

KurseongNo matter when you visit Kurseong, you will always be welcomed by a pleasant climate. The name Kurseong is derived from Kharsang meaning the Land of White orchids. This beautiful town is about 47 km from Siliguri and 30 km from Darjeeling; so even if you are just passing by, do pay a quick visit to soak up the fresh air and leave feeling fresh and energised.

Nabadwip

Nabadwip is the birthplace of the great religious leader, Chaitanya Mahapravu. On visiting, you will find yourself among the scattered forests of pomegranate trees and orchards of several kinds of fruits, including mango, bael and jackfruit. The roads will lead you to quaint villages steeped in religious tradition.

Raichak

Raichak is an absolute treat for anyone looking for a quick weekend getaway. This is a small town offering a spectacular view of the river Ganges. It has a peaceful atmosphere that will surely make you feel calm and collected
