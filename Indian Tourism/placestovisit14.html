Places to visit

Uttar Pradesh has various visitor attractions including within the city.

Uttar Pradesh

Varanasi

Varanasi is often hailed as India's spiritual capital and is also one of the oldest cities in the world. This ancient city of Varanasi is dispersed among a maze of winding avenues along the western banks of the Ganges.Furthermore, Varanasi is surrounded by temples practically everywhere you look, but the Kashi Vishwanath Temple is the most popular and the oldest among them.

Agra

AgraAgra is home to the Taj Mahal, one of the Seven Wonders of the World, and is also considered one of the most beautiful places in Uttar Pradesh. Additionally, Agra is also a must-visit for anyone who lives in or travels to India because of its extraordinary amalgamation of history and architecture that gives the city its enchantment.

Lucknow

LucknowLucknow, the largest and capital city of Uttar Pradesh, is located along the Gomti River. In a sentence, Lucknow is a city of kebabs and nawabs, a destination of architecture, history, literature, and culture.This creative centre of the Awadh area also skilfully unites the grandeur of a sparkling past and the elegance of a modern city, combining a rich colonial heritage with sophisticated museums.

Vrindavan

VrindavanVrindavan is one of the oldest settlements on the Yamuna River and is also regarded as one of the most significant pilgrimage centres for Krishna devotees. It is believed that Lord Krishna spent his formative years at Vrindavan. So, people flock to Vrindavan in great numbers for spiritual enlightenment since it is hailed as a sacred destination in Hinduism.

Allahabad

AllahabadThe city of Allahabad, which is now formally referred to as Prayagraj, is a popular destination in Uttar Pradesh. The Maha Kumbh Mela, which is the largest Hindu gathering on the banks of the Sangam, has been hosted in Allahabad. Although Allahabad is frequently overlooked in favour of more accessible cities, it can offer much more than just religious significance.

Ayodhya

AyodhyaAyodhya, situated in Uttar Pradesh along the Sarayu River, is considered one of the seven holy cities for Hindus. Since Ayodhya is thought to be the location of Lord Rama's birth, it plays a significant part in the religious beliefs of Hindus.Additionally, four of Jainism's 24 Tirthankaras were born in this sacred town, which is also a reason that draws tourists with its tranquil ghats from different parts of the world.

Kanpur

KanpurKanpur is the largest city in the U.P. and is the hub of industrial and commercial activity. It was formerly referred to as the Manchester of India, but currently, it serves as Uttar Pradesh's business hub. Above all, Kanpur is one of North India's principal industrial epicentres and has its own historical, religious, and commercial significance.

Noida

NoidaNoida is a masterfully built city with amenities for everyone, including parks, incredible street cuisine, upscale restaurants, shopping malls, sports facilities, etc. Additionally, speaking of the climate, it has hot summers and chilling winters, although the temperature is mild from August to November.

Hastinapur

HastinapurAn ancient town Hastinapur served as the focal point for the glorious stories of the Mahabharata. Additionally, Hastinapur is also a pilgrimage site and is located on the banks of the Ganga close to Meerut. This place is also popular among Jains, as it is the birthplace of three Jain Tirthankaras.

Meerut

MeerutIn Meerut, you'll find a vibrant culture, breathtaking architecture, interesting history, and mouthwatering food. This ancient city is a part of the National Capital Region and is situated roughly 70 kilometres away from New Delhi (Delhi-NCR).In Meerut, there is a fascinating representation of history, a thriving modern art scene, and culture, which draws tourists around the globe.
