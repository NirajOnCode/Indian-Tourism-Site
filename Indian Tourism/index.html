India

India, located in South Asia, is the seventh-largest country by land area and the second-most populous country in the world, with over 1.4 billion people. Known for its rich cultural heritage, India boasts a diverse history shaped by various dynasties, religions, and traditions. It is the birthplace of major religions such as Hinduism, Buddhism, Jainism, and Sikhism.

According to the Ministry of Tourism, over 6.19 million and 1.52 million foreign tourists arrived in India in 2022 and 2021 respectively compared to 10.93 million in 2019, representing a -44% degrowth.[5][6] In 2022, Gujarat Tops India in Foreign Tourism with 20.17% Share in 2022. Gujarat rose as a global tourism hotspot continues with over 15.40 lakh tourists visiting in 2023.[7] In 2023, 2509.63 million Domestic Tourist Visits (DTVs) were recorded (provisionally) compared to 1731.01 Million DTVs in 2022

India is a land of many different languages and many different religions such as Buddhism, Jainism, Islam, Hinduism, etc. There are various food styles and dressing styles depending on the regions of the country, setting the perfect example of Unity in Diversity.

The World Economic Forum's Travel and Tourism Development Index 2021, which replaced its previous Travel and Tourism Competitiveness Report, ranks India 54th out of 117 countries overall.[9] The last edition of the Travel and Tourism Competitiveness Report, published in 2019, had ranked India 34th out of 140 countries overall. The report ranked the price competitiveness of India's tourism sector 13th out of 140 countries. It mentioned that India has quite good air transport infrastructure (ranked 33rd), particularly given the country's stage of development, and reasonable ground and port infrastructure (ranked 28th). .

Tourism has played a very vital role in reviving India’s art and culture. Tourists from foreign countries show a keen interest in the rich culture and heritage of India and this boosts the morale of the small artisans and adds to their novelty.

Places to visit

jammu-kashmir

Jammu and Kashmir is the northernmost state of the Union of India. The state shares international border with China and Afghanistan in the north, Tibet in the east and Pakistan in the west. The states of Punjab and Himachal Pradesh form its southern border.

Himachal Pradesh

Himachal Pradesh is a state in the northern part of India, situated in the Western Himalayas. It is characterized by an extreme landscape featuring several peaks and extensive river systems. Himachal Pradesh is the northernmost state of India and shares borders with the union territories of Jammu and Kashmir and Ladakh to the north, and the states of Punjab to the west, Haryana to the southwest, Uttarakhand to the southeast, and a very narrow border with Uttar Pradesh to the south. The state also shares an international border to the east with the Tibet Autonomous Region in China.

  • Learn More

Punjab

Punjab is a geopolitical, cultural, and historical region in South Asia, located in the northwestern part of the Indian subcontinent, comprising areas of modern-day eastern Pakistan and northwestern India. It is known as the Land of the Five Rivers, referring to the Jhelum, Chenab, Ravi, Beas, and Sutlej rivers, which are tributaries of the Indus River.

  • Learn More

Rajasthan

Rajasthan, known as the "Land of Kings" and India's largest desert state, is a captivating destination in northern India that mesmerise travellers from around the world. The state presents a harmonious blend of lively cities, hospitable locals, exquisite cuisine, majestic palaces, and grand forts. Rajasthan offers a perfect travel experience, seamlessly fusing timeless allure with contemporary charm. This enchanting state beckons you with a symphony of vibrant cultures, a canvas painted with tales of valour, and landscapes that will steal your heart.

  • Learn More

Gujarat

Gujarat's capital city is Gandhinagar, while its largest city is Ahmedabad.The state is renowned for its beaches, temples, historic capitals, wildlife sanctuaries, hill resorts, sculptures, handicrafts, arts, and festivals.It is also home to 23 sites of the ancient Indus Valley Civilization.

  • Learn More

Maharastra

Maharashtra is one of India’s largest commercial and industrial centres, which has led to its being called the gateway of India. Maharashtra is also famous for its culture and beauty. The ancient cave paintings found at Ajanta and Ellora are UNESCO World Heritage sites and popular tourist destinations. Music, dance, Bollywood, festivals, and much more contribute to this lively culture.

  • Learn More

Delhi

Delhi, officially the National Capital Territory (NCT) of Delhi, is a city and a union territory of India containing New Delhi, the capital of India. It straddles the Yamuna river and is spread chiefly to the west, or beyond its right bank. Delhi shares borders with the state of Uttar Pradesh in the east and with the state of Haryana in the remaining directions.

  • Learn More

Goa

Goa is known for its pristine beaches and is a popular tourist destination, attracting both international and domestic visitors.The state has a rich cultural heritage influenced by Portuguese rule, which lasted for about 450 years.The official language is Konkani, but Marathi and English are also widely spoken. Tourism in Goa is primarily focused on the coastal areas, with popular attractions including beaches, historical sites, and natural beauty.The state has a diverse flora and fauna, lying close to the North Western Ghats rainforests, one of the world's rare biodiversity hotspots.

  • Learn More

Uttarakhand

Uttarakhand is a state in northern India, known for its natural beauty and religious significance. It was formed on November 9, 2000, as a result of the division of the state of Uttar Pradesh. The state is bordered by Himachal Pradesh to the northwest, Tibet to the north, Nepal to the east, and Uttar Pradesh to the south and southeast. Dehradun serves as the state capital, with Nainital being the judicial capital.

  • Learn More

Tamil Nadu

Tamil Nadu is the southernmost state of India, located in South India with a rich cultural heritage and diverse geography. It is the home of the Tamil people and their language, Tamil, which is one of the longest surviving classical languages in the world.The state is known for its ancient temples, vibrant festivals, and classical dance forms like Bharatanatyam.Tamil Nadu's capital and largest city is Chennai, formerly known as Madras.The state is bounded by the Bay of Bengal to the east and the Indian Ocean to the south, and it shares borders with the Indian states of Kerala, Karnataka, and Andhra Pradesh

  • Learn More

Kerala

Kerala is a state located in the southwestern part of India, known for its unique cultural and geographical characteristics.It stretches for about 360 miles along the Malabar Coast, varying in width from roughly 20 to 75 miles. The state is bordered by Karnataka to the north and northeast, Tamil Nadu to the east and south, and the Lakshadweep Sea to the west

Bihar

Bihar is a state located in the eastern part of India, bordered by Nepal to the north, West Bengal to the northeast, Jharkhand to the south, and Uttar Pradesh to the west.It is the third most populous state in India, with a population exceeding 103 million people.The state capital is Patna, which was formerly known as Patliputra.

  • Learn More

Arunachal Pradesh

Arunachal Pradesh is a state in northeast India, known as the "Land of the Dawn-lit Mountains" due to its location on the northeastern tip of India, where the sun first rises.It borders the Indian states of Assam and Nagaland to the south, and shares international borders with Bhutan in the west, Myanmar in the east, and China's Tibet Autonomous Region in the north.

Uttar Pradesh

Uttar Pradesh is a state in northern India, known for its rich history, cultural heritage, and religious significance. It is the most populous state in India with a population of nearly 241 million people as of 2021.The state is bordered by Nepal to the north, and other Indian states including Rajasthan, Haryana, Himachal Pradesh, Uttarakhand, Madhya Pradesh, Chhattisgarh, Jharkhand, and Bihar

West Bengal

Indian Tourism

India is a vast and diverse country in South Asia, known for its rich history, culture, and traditions. It is the world’s most populous country, with a population of over 1.45 billion people. India is a federal parliamentary democratic republic, consisting of 28 states and 8 Union Territories.The country is famous for its ancient civilization, with contributions in philosophy, science, arts, and literature. Its capital is New Delhi, and the official language is Hindi, with English widely used in government and business. India is a fast-growing economy and a major player in global affairs.
