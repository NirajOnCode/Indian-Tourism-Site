Places to visit

Arunachal-Pradesh has various visitor attractions including within the city.

Arunachal-Pradesh

Tawang Monastery

Tawang MonasteryDo not forget to visit India’s largest monastery in Tawang, one of the most beautiful and culturally rich places in Arunachal Pradesh. It was part of Tibet until the 20th century, but now it is in the Indian city of Tawang. This monastery is known as Golden Namgyal Lhatse, which means ‘celestial paradise in a clear night’. It is the largest in the country and the second largest in the world. Arunachal Pradesh is a delight; it works so well to preserve its culture, people, flora and fauna. So, visit Tawang In Arunachal Pradesh for a wholesome experience.

Daporijo

DaporijoNestled in the lush and verdant hills, Daporoji is a picturesque village offering stunning views of the surroundings. As a cultural enthusiast, you can enjoy witnessing culture through local festivals and customs. Additionally, the flowing streams, dense forests, and wildlife make the city one of the best places to visit in Arunachal Pradesh, especially for wildlife enthusiasts and nature lovers. Remember to check out the best homestays in Arunachal Pradesh for comfortable stays.

Hayuliang Village

Hayuliang VillageStep into one of the famous villages of Arunachal Pradesh and say goodbye to the crowded destinations. Hayuliang in Arunachal Pradesh is known for its rolling hills and meandering rovers. It is home to the Mishmi tribe, whose festivals and traditions add allure to the region. Here, you can trek and take nature walks with your loved ones. The scenic environment and greenery make this village the must-visit tourist destination in Arunachal Pradesh.

Gorichen Peak

Gorichen PeakAs a trekker and mountaineer, you can have an adrenaline-fueled experience while trekking to the famous Gorichen Peak. The trek usually takes about 10-14 days, including time for acclimatization and exploring the surrounding areas. Standing at 6,588 metres, the Gorichen Peak is the highest mountain in Arunachal Pradesh. Enjoy challenging climbs and stunning vistas during the escapade. So, unleash your inner adventurer and trek to Gorichen Peak for an adventurous time.

Madhuri lake

Madhuri LakeAlso known as Shungetser Lake, Madhuri Lake is a pristine glacial lake nestled amidst the terrain of the eastern Himalayas and dense forests. If you are an adventurer or a nature enthusiast, take a challenging hike through the panoramic landscapes to reach the lake. Enjoy opportunities for wildlife sightings and experience the tranquillity of high altitudes. As a couple or family, you can have a mesmerizing experience at Madhuri Lake and the journey of reaching it.

Golden pagoda Namsai

Golden pagoda NamsaiIt is a magnificent Buddhist temple, inviting all to seek blessings and say prayers. Golden Pagoda Namsai is renowned for its intricate golden exterior and design. The temple stands as a symbol of spiritual heritage and peace. Glimpse into Buddhist religion and traditions by exploring the mandir and praying to God. The pagoda is among the most-visited places in Arunachal Pradesh among locals and tourists for its religious significance and architecture.

Tirap

TirapTirap is the perfect tourist place in Arunachal Pradesh, especially for someone who loves nature and adventurous activities. The remote district offers thrilling opportunities for thrill and outdoor activities, such as nature walks in dense forests and trekking. The Tiral River adds a scenic touch to the trek, perfect for relaxation. You can even encounter wildlife and take photographs for a wholesome experience. So, add Tirap to the must-visit Arunachal Pradesh destinations list for your 7 nights tour.

Yupia

YupiaEnter the scenic town of Yupia, which is highly known for its serene aura and cultural significance. It serves as a gateway to exploring the natural allure and heritage. The rolling hills and lush greenery set a perfect backdrop for travellers to relax and rejuvenate. If you seek escape from urban life and have a peaceful time with your loved ones, add Yupia to the list of places to see in Arunachal Pradesh. Lastly, there are many things to do in Arunachal Pradesh, but exploring the towns gives you an overall glimpse of the region.

Yingkiong

YingkiongIf you wish to explore the local culture, enter Yingkiong. Visit tribal villages and experience their customs while sightseeing the picturesque landscapes. The area is known for its trekking trails and natural beauty so that you can have a thrilling time. Escape from the cityscapes and bustle and soak yourself in the natural vistas for rejuvenation and comfort. Give a unique turn to your vacation by discovering villages in the city and having the perfect time. Siang River is among the best places to visit in Pasighat too. However, Yingkiong and Pasighat are two different towns. So, do not get confused.

Upper Dibang Valley

Upper Dibang ValleyUpper Dibang Valley is one of the top places in Arunachal Pradesh. However, it is a little bit peculiar. It is the largest district in India, with an area of 9,129 sq km, but it is also the least populated district in the country. Strange, right? The Native Idu Mishmis inhibited this place, and then there were a few more tribes whose nativity was from Tibet. When you come here, you must reach Anini, as this is the only place with connections to the rest of the country. The main attraction in Dibang Valley is a wildlife sanctuary.

Arunachal-PradeshArunachal Pradesh is a state in northeast India, known as the "Land of the Dawn-lit Mountains" due to its location on the northeastern tip of India, where the sun first rises.It borders the Indian states of Assam and Nagaland to the south, and shares international borders with Bhutan in the west, Myanmar in the east, and China's Tibet Autonomous Region in the north.
